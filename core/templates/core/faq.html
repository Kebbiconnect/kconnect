Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Kebbi Progressive Network

The Kebbi Progressive Network (KPN) is a state-level digital platform designed to strengthen youth mobilization, promote charity and welfare activities, empower citizens through educational and economic initiatives, and advocate for civic responsibility and good governance across Kebbi State.

Our motto: "One Voice, One Change"

To join KPN:

  1. Fill out the registration form on our website
  2. Follow our official Facebook page (required step)
  3. Wait for approval if applying for a leadership position
  4. General membership is automatically approved after Facebook verification

Note: Following our Facebook page is mandatory for all members.

Yes, membership in KPN is completely free. There are no membership fees or hidden charges.

However, we welcome voluntary donations to support our community development activities and programs.

KPN has a hierarchical leadership structure:

  • State Executive Council: 20 positions (including President, Secretaries, Treasurer, etc.)
  • Zonal Coordinators: 3 positions per zone (3 zones total)
  • LGA Leaders: 10 positions per LGA (21 LGAs total)
  • Ward Leaders: 8 positions per ward (225 wards total)
  • General Members: Unlimited

Leadership applications require approval from higher-level supervisors:

  • State Executives: Approved by President
  • Zonal Coordinators: Approved by State Executives
  • LGA Leaders: Approved by Zonal Coordinators
  • Ward Leaders: Approved by LGA Leaders

Applications are reviewed based on commitment, qualifications, and availability of positions.

KPN leaders have specific duties including:

  • Staff management and approval of subordinate applications
  • Organizing events and activities in their jurisdiction
  • Maintaining duty logs and accountability records
  • Engaging with the official Facebook page
  • Promoting KPN values and objectives in their communities
  • Regular reporting to supervisors

KPN organizes various activities including:

  • Youth Mobilization: Training and empowerment programs
  • Welfare Activities: Support for vulnerable communities
  • Advocacy Campaigns: Promoting good governance and transparency
  • Educational Initiatives: Skill development and civic education
  • Community Development: Projects benefiting local communities
  • Networking Events: Connecting members across all LGAs

As a KPN member, you can participate by:

  • Following our official Facebook page for announcements
  • Attending events organized in your zone, LGA, or ward
  • Volunteering for community development projects
  • Sharing our content and campaigns on social media
  • Applying for leadership positions to organize activities

You can support KPN through offline donations to our Nigerian bank accounts:

  • Visit our Support Us page for complete bank details
  • Include "KPN DONATION" in your transaction reference
  • Notify us through our Facebook page after making a donation
  • All contributions support community development initiatives

You can reach us through:

  • Facebook: @kpnkebbi
  • Email: kpn2020@gmail.com
  • Website Contact Form: Contact Us page

We typically respond within 24-48 hours.

Following our official Facebook page is required because:

  • It's our primary communication channel for announcements and updates
  • It helps verify member authenticity and prevents fake registrations
  • Leaders are expected to engage with official content as part of their duties
  • It builds a unified community presence across all social media platforms
  • It ensures members stay informed about important KPN activities and initiatives

KPN is a non-partisan organization focused on:

  • Good governance advocacy (regardless of political party)
  • Youth empowerment and civic education
  • Community development and welfare activities
  • Transparency and accountability in public service

We welcome members from all political backgrounds and focus on issues that benefit all citizens of Kebbi State.
